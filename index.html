Raouf Kerkouche

Short Bio:

I am a final year PhD student at Inria Grenoble center in the Privatics team. My research interests are at the intersection of privacy, security and machine learning. My thesis is supervised by Dr. Claude Castelluccia (Privatics team-Inria) and Dr. Pierre Genevès (LIG and Tyrex team-Inria). I am also working with Dr. Gergely Ács. Recently, I was affiliated with the Chair on the Legal and Regulatory Implications of Artificial Intelligence.
Before starting my thesis, I had the privilege of working with Prof. Mérouane Debbah on establishment of a mathematical algorithm for Content Recommendation and Prof. Patrick Maillé on Multi-Armed Bandit algorithms for Node-based optimization of LoRa transmissions.

My main areas of research are:

  • Privacy-preserving machine learning
  • Differentially Private Federated, Decentralized and Centralized learning
  • Secure and Robust machine learning
  • Applications to real-world datasets
  • Game theory
  • Contribution measurement methods for participants in Federated/Decentralized learning
  • Bandwidth-Efficient Federated/Decentralized Learning
  • Fairness in machine learning
